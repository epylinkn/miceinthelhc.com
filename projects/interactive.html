Portal

 

Build at Bombay Beach   Read

Once a thriving vacation destination, Bombay Beach is now in rapid decay, full of rusted trailers, stray dogs, and a dwindling population. In recent years, a new group of artists have begun transforming abandoned properties into installations and regenerating interest in the town.

Just outside the town sits a span of bare desert, surrounded by the shores of the Salton Sea and the awe-inspiring San Andreas Mountains, where Portal was placed.

Sitting between sea and mountains, Portal was a meeting point of realities, enabling different perspectives into digital and real landscapes by acting as an open canvas for both humans and the desert.

As a temporary structure composed of warehouse shelving material lined with networked televisions running open-source software, Portal was both functional and modular. Over the course of three months, it served as a vehicle for artistic dialogue with nature, community activities, and an observation deck to witness the change of seasons in the desert.

To inaugurate Portal, JOYLAB created a multichannel, experimental AR video piece to screen across its televisions. “Freyja” depicts ennui emerging from digital selves while nature retreats and a post-apocalyptic cyber-feminist landscape takes its place. By using real-time AR objects in the landscape, the piece explores the spatial qualities of impending digital realities within nature.

Cloud Bathroom

 

Build for Luxury Escapism   Read

Cloud Bathroom is a break from a break, a space within a space. By filling the restroom of a windowless basement with a sky full of clouds, going to the bathroom becomes an act of liberation and creates room for imagination. Functionally, the piece was designed to add additional privacy through signage, barriers, and sound design, without damaging the building.

When needing to freshen up, a guest heads to the restroom, encountering a doorway and an occupied sign with a cloud perched on top. As they enter through the doorway, the sign illuminates, and they pass through a hallway featuring circular windows with a view flying through the clouds. When finally reaching the bathroom, the lock of the door illuminates a hanging cloud as the sound of rushing wind sweeps by. When the guest runs the sink to wash their hands, the sound of wind gives way to rain as the lights ripple with drops of water. Upon returning to the hallway, birds chirp and a rainbow of lights guides them to the exit.
